Claypot Rice Recipe

Time: 1 hour

Difficulty: Hard

Serves: 1

Description

Claypot Rice (or bo zai fan in Cantonese) is a humble rich dish with an incredibly deep and complex flavor profile. We can trace the modern claypot rice dish to ancient China where people used claypots over fire, traditionally charcoal to cook rice. By using a claypot, we can infuse the rice with rich flavors, unlike in modern rice cookers because rice in a claypot can "breathe" through pores in the clay. In Hong Kong, street vendor chefs prepare a classic claypot rice steaming scallions, ginger, Gailan, marinated chicken, marinated mushrooms, and Chinese Sausage while simultaneously cooking the rice below. In doing so, the flavor from the toppings slowly seeps into the rice to develop a gently seasoned bed of fluffy white rice. The most iconic component of the dish and the most technically challenging part of the dish sits at the bottom of the bowl: the charred rice. Chefs must carefully rotate the bowl to char the rice at the bottom of the claypot so that a crispy layer can form. The timing must be perfect. If the rice is not charred enough, the iconic crunchy rice will not form. But if the rice is charred too long, the rice will burn and taint the smell of the dish. For this recipe, I will cover the core elements of a classic claypot rice and provide guidance as to how you can mimic the flavors of Hong Kong street food at home. I hope you enjoy!

Notes Before Starting

  • I would suggest getting a claypot especially if you have a gas stove. You can also find other claypots that work on induction stoves. If you don't have a claypot and don't wish to buy one, you can use a small (~2-3 Qt) nonstick pot with a lid.

  • I would suggest you read through the entire recipe at least once before attempting to cook this dish it can be technically challenging.

  • There are times where you might want to soak or marinade ingredients while you prepare other ingredients.

  • Start an hour before serving!

Preparation

  1. Soak the claypot in water
  2. Set claypot on the stove with the heat turned to low
  3. Over a period of 15 minutes, slowly increase the heat of the stove until you see the water begin to boil. If you increase the heat too quickly, you could crack the pot.

Cooking

  1. Drain the water from the rice
  2. Layer the rice on the bottom of the claypot
  3. Fill with enough water so that the rice is submerged 0.5 cm.
  4. Cover the claypot and cook for (XXX) minutes or until you see that the rice is no longer submerged by the water
  5. Gently stir rice
  6. Layer the Chinese sausage, chicken, and mushrooms on top of the rice
  7. Cover the claypot and cook for (XXX) minutes

Finishing

  1. Quickly remove lid and...
  2. Layer the green onions on top of the sausage, chicken, and mushrooms
  3. Layer the ginger on top of the green onions
  4. Cover claypot
  5. Listen for the crackle!
  6. Slowly rotate/angle pot to ensure even distrubution

Serving

Served immediately (hot) in a claypot. You can either directly eat out of a claypot or scoop into a separate bowl.

Storing

Store leftovers in an airtight container and refrigerate. Rice remains good for 2-3 days. Cover with a wet paper towel and heat until hot after refrigerating.
