Claypot Rice

Description

Claypot Rice (or bo zai fan in Cantonese) is a humble rich dish with an incredibly deep and complex flavor profile. We can trace the modern claypot rice dish to ancient China where people used claypots over fire, traditionally charcoal to cook rice. By using a claypot, we can infuse the rice with rich flavors, unlike in modern rice cookers because rice in a claypot can "breathe" through pores in the clay. In Hong Kong, street vendor chefs prepare a classic claypot rice steaming scallions, ginger, Gailan, marinated chicken, marinated mushrooms, and Chinese Sausage while simultaneously cooking the rice below. In doing so, the flavor from the toppings slowly seep into the rice to develop a gently season bed of fluffy white rice. The most iconic component of the dish and the most technically challenging part of the dish sits at the bottom of the bowl: the charred rice. Chefs must carefully rotate the bowl to char the rice at the bottom of the claypot so that a crispy layer can form. The timing must be perfect. If the rice is not charred enough, the iconic crunchy rice will not form. But if the rice is charred too long, the rice will burn and taint the smell of the dish.

For this recipe, I will cover the core elements of a classic claypot rice and provide guidance as to how you can mimic the flavors of Hong Kong with limited equipment!

Instructions

  1. Mise en Place
    1. Prepare the Rice
      1. Wash the rice
      2. Soak the rice in hot water for 10 minutes
    2. Prepare the Chinese Sausage
      1. Soak the Chinese Sausage in hot water for 10 minutes
      2. Cut the Chinese Sausage into thin slices
    3. Marinate the Chicken and Mushrooms
      1. Cut the chicken into 1-in pieces
      2. Marinate the chicken and mushrooms with Soy Sauce (XXX), Dark Soy Sauce (XXX), Cornstarch (XXX), Cooking Wine (XXX), white pepper (XXX)
      3. Let sit for at least (XXX) minutes
    4. Misc vegetables
      1. Thinly slice the green onions
      2. Thinly slice the ginger
      3. Wash and cut Gailan
  2. (If you have a claypot) Prepare the Claypot
    1. Soak the claypot in water
    2. Set claypot on the stove with the heat turned to low
    3. Over a period of 15 minutes, slowly increase the heat of the stove until you see the water begin to boil. If you increase the heat too quickly, you could crack the pot.
  3. Assembling the ingredients in the Claypot
    1. Drain the water from the rice
    2. Layer the rice on the bottom of the claypot
    3. Fill with (XXX) water or enough water so that the rice is submerged 0.5 cm.
    4. Cover the claypot and cook for (XXX) minutes or until you see that the rice is no longer submerged by the water
    5. Gently stir rice
    6. Layer the Chinese sausage, chicken, and mushrooms on top of the rice
    7. Cover the claypot and cook for (XXX) minutes
  4. Finish the Claypot Rice
    1. Quickly remove lid and...
    2. Layer the green onions on top of the sausage, chicken, and mushrooms
    3. Layer the ginger on top of the green onions
    4. Cover claypot
    5. Listen for the crackle!
    6. Slowly rotate/angle pot to ensure even distrubution
