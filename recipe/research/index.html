Claypot Rice

Claypot Rice (or bo zai fan in Cantonese) is a rice dish with a humble appearance and an incredibly deep and complex flavor profile. We can trace the modern claypot rice dish to ancient China where people used clay pots over fire, traditionally charcoal to cook rice. By using a clay pot, we can infuse the rice with richer flavors, simply because the rice can "breathe", that are typically lost in modern rice cookers when making rice. In Hong Kong, street vendor chefs top a classic clay pot rice dish with scallions, ginger, Gailan, marinated chicken, marinated mushrooms, and Chinese Sausage while cooking the rice beneath. In doing so, the flavor from the toppings slowly seep into the rice to develop a gently season bed of fluffy white rice. The most iconic component of the dish and the most technically challenging part of the dish sits at the bottom of the bowl: the charred rice. Chefs must carefully rotate the bowl to char the rice at the bottom of the claypot so that a crispy layer can form. The timing must be perfect. If the rice is not charred enough, the iconic crunchy rice will not form. But if the rice is charred too long, the rice will burn and taint the smell of the dish.

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Claypot
    1. Soak the in water
    2. Set claypot on the stove with the heat turned to low
    3. Incrementally heat the claypot
  2. Mise en Place
    1. Prepare the Rice
      1. Wash the rice
      2. Soak the rice in hot water for 10 minutes
    2. Prepare the Chinese Sausage
      1. Soak the Chinese Sausage in hot water for 10 minutes
      2. Cut the Chinese Sausage into thin slices
    3. Marinate the Chicken and Mushrooms
      1. Cut the chicken into 1-in pieces
      2. Marinate the chicken and mushrooms with Soy Sauce (XXX), Dark Soy Sauce (XXX), Cornstarch (XXX), Cooking Wine (XXX), white pepper (XXX)
      3. Let sit for at least (XXX) minutes
    4. Misc vegetables
      1. Thinly slice the green onions
      2. Thinly slice the ginger
      3. Wash and cut Gailan
  3. Assembling the Claypot
    1. Drain the water from the rice
    2. Layer the rice on the bottom of the claypot
    3. Fill with (XXX) water or enough water so that the rice is submerged 0.5 cm.
    4. Cover the claypot and cook for (XXX) minutes or until you see that the rice is no longer submerged by the water
    5. Gently stir rice
    6. Layer the Chinese sausage, chicken, and mushrooms on top of the rice
    7. Cover the claypot and cook for (XXX) minutes
  4. Finish the Claypot Rice
    1. Quickly remove lid and...
    2. Layer the green onions on top of the sausage, chicken, and mushrooms
    3. Layer the ginger on top of the green onions
    4. Cover claypot
    5. Listen for the crackle!
    6. Slowly rotate/angle pot to ensure even distrubution
